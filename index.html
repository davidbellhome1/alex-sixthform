Four routes. One Alex.

Gordon's runs 24 A Levels and a couple of BTECs. Here are the four routes through that menu that actually fit what you want.

You want to run your own water park one day. You want to work with animals, possibly at Disney's Animal Kingdom. You don't want to sit at a desk. You like drama, you're flying in Business, and you and Matthew are setting up a reselling business on Whatnot.

Good starting point. That tells me a lot of the standard A Level advice doesn't apply to you, so here are the four routes through Gordon's that I think do.

None of them is perfect. Read them, push back, tell me where I've got you wrong.

How Gordon's Sixth Form works
  • 3 main subjects (A Level or BTEC) plus 1 supplementary "+1" in Year 12 only.
  • BTEC Business counts as one A Level. BTEC Sport (Diploma) counts as two.
  • EPQ is the most popular +1. You pick the topic. 5,000 words. Up to 28 UCAS points at A*.
  • Not on the menu: Politics, Religious Studies (full A Level), Philosophy, Law, Travel & Tourism, IB.
  • A Level entry: 5+ GCSEs at grade 5, including English and Maths at 5.
  • BTEC entry: 5+ GCSEs at grade 4, including English and Maths at 4.

Path 01 · Academic + Entrepreneurial

The Water Park Founder

Business · Geography · Drama · EPQ

Business A Level Geography A Level Drama A Level + EPQ

Built around the water park ambition. Business is your strongest GCSE by a distance. Keep going with it. Geography matters more than you'd think for a leisure business: site selection, weather, demographics, planning, environmental impact. Drama is the one that surprises people. If you run a business one day, you spend most of your time pitching, persuading and presenting customers and staff. Drama is the only A Level that explicitly trains that.

For your EPQ, pick something like "a feasibility study for a UK family water park". Real 5,000-word project. Useful piece of work to walk into a UCAS interview with.

Opens
Business and management degrees. Real estate and property development. Hospitality and leisure management. Degree apprenticeships at Center Parcs, Merlin, Whitbread, IHG. Pretty much any non-STEM university route.
Closes
Anything science-heavy. If you decide later you want vet science, zoology or marine biology, this isn't the stack.

The Animal Kingdom Route

Biology · Business · Geography · EPQ

Biology A Level Business A Level Geography A Level + EPQ

Pick this if animals genuinely sit at the centre of what you want, not as part of the leisure venue. Zookeeping, conservation, marine biology and zoology degrees all want Biology A Level. So do most direct animal-care jobs.

Honest problem. Your Biology working grade is 4. Target is 7/8. You'd need real progress in Year 11 to start the A Level on stable footing. Possible, but Biology has to become something you actively work on rather than something you tolerate.

Path 03 · Hybrid · my pick if forced to choose

The Experience Designer

Business · Drama · BTEC Sport (Double) · EPQ

Business A Level Drama A Level BTEC Sport (Double) + EPQ

Hear me out before you bin this one. "BTEC Sport" sounds like it's for athletes. The way Gordon's runs it, it isn't. Of the nine units they teach, only one needs you to actually perform, and Gordon's doesn't even pick that one. The rest is sports psychology, leadership, customer behaviour, business in sport, and health and safety. The big external exam at the end is a 3-hour case study about running a leisure business. It's a Business and Leisure Management qualification with the wrong name on the cover.

Stack it with Business A Level and Drama A Level and you've got the three sides of running a venue: commercial, customer-facing, and operational. Mostly coursework, lighter on exams than three A Levels, which suits you better. If I had to bet on what you're doing in ten years, you're running an attraction, a leisure destination, or an experience venue. This is the stack that points there.

Opens
Leisure management. Event management. Hospitality. Theme park operations. Merlin, Disney and Universal management trainee schemes. Sports business. Experience design. Your own venture eventually. Most degree apprenticeships fit this stack.
Closes
Most science-led routes. Some Russell Group academic degrees. But you weren't aiming there anyway.

The Fast-Lane Vocational

BTEC Business · BTEC Sport (Double) · Drama · Sports Leaders

BTEC Business BTEC Sport (Double) Drama A Level + Sports Leaders

Most coursework-heavy of the four. Almost no exams, assessment by portfolio and project. Lines you up for a degree apprenticeship at 18 with someone like Center Parcs, Merlin, Whitbread, GLL or a major hotel group. Paid job, real responsibility, qualification at the end, no student debt.

For your goals specifically, this could be the best route, not the backup. Worth taking on its own merits. The version to seriously consider if Year 11 doesn't close the focus gap and the working grades stay below target, but worth a look either way.

Opens
Every leisure, hospitality, sport and business degree apprenticeship in the country. Direct entry into industry at 18. Faster route to commercial responsibility. University still possible later via degree apprenticeship.
Closes
Top-tier traditional universities for any subject. Vet science, zoology, marine biology. Anything that wants pure A Levels.

Year 11 decides which side of the A Level / BTEC line you start from. The single thing to fix is focus, flagged in 8 out of 10 subjects last term. Sort that out and most of these grades will move on their own. Paths 03 and 04 stay open either way. Path 01 needs grade 5s across the board. Path 02 needs Biology to come up properly.

The +1 pick

EPQ. No question. You choose the topic. 5,000 words. Worth up to 28 UCAS points if you get an A*.

Topic options for you: a feasibility study for a UK family water park, a comparison of Center Parcs and Disney's Animal Kingdom as business models, or the economics of immersive animal experiences. Use it as a real piece of work. I'll show you how to use AI properly through the project.

If Path 04 ends up the route, swap EPQ for the Sports Leaders Award Level 3. Both worth UCAS points, both deliver something concrete.

Where I land

If I had to pick one, it's Path 03.

Path 03 plays to your actual strengths and lines up with the career you keep describing, the one where you're running a place rather than working in someone else's office.

Path 01 is the more academic version of the same direction. Path 02 is only right if animals are the centre rather than part of the leisure offer. Path 04 is the strongest fit if Year 11 doesn't close the grade gap.
